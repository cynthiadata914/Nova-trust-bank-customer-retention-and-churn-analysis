# Nova-trust-bank-customer-retention-and-churn-analysis
## Project overview
Customer churn is a critical challenge in the banking industry, directly impacting revenue, customer lifetime value, and growth.
This project analyzes customer behavior and attrition patterns for Nova-trust Bank to identify the key drivers of churn and provide actionable insights for improving customer retention.
Using SQL for exploration, Excel for feature engineering, and Power BI for visualization, i built an interactive dashboard that enables stakeholders to monitor churn trends across demographics, credit risk segments, products, and regions.

## Business Objectives
- Measure overall customer churn rate
- Identify high-risk customer segments
- Understand how tenure, products, and credit scores influence churn
- Compare churn patterns across countries and demographics
- Support data driven retention strategies

## Key metrics tracked
- Total customers
- Churned customers
- Churned rate (%)
- Average account balance
- Average credit score
- Churn by age group
- Churn by tenure group
- Churn by product type
- Churn by country

## Key insights
- Overall churn rate stands at 20%, indicating significant retention opportunities
- Customers with short tenure show the highest likelihood of churning
- Senior age group exhibits the highest churn rate among demographics
- Customers with fewer products are more likely to leave the bank
- Certain countries show concentrated churn hotspots
- Higher credit scores correlate with better retention
